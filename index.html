⚠ CKD Research Update — 2025

The original "no CKD" contraindication is being actively re-evaluated. Two 2025 studies (Heerspink et al., Kidney Int Reports; TRANSCEND-CKD trial, Nephrol Dial Transplant) found Retatrutide at higher doses (8–12 mg) reduced albuminuria by 28–37% vs. placebo and preserved or improved eGFR in T2D and obesity populations.

The TRANSCEND-CKD trial (NCT05936151) specifically enrolled CKD patients with eGFR 25–75 ml/min/1.73 m² — which includes Stage 3b. Results pending. The large outcomes trial TRIUMPH-Outcomes (NCT06383390) completes ~2029.

For CKD patients: Physician and ideally nephrologist supervision is essential before starting. Regular eGFR monitoring required. An initial eGFR dip at ~week 12 is expected and documented — it typically reverses by week 24.
